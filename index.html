Light pollution is caused by the excessive or incorrect use of artificial lighting. One of the most prevalent displays of light pollution is skyglow, where light bounces or is directed straight into the sky, ultimately diminishing our ability to see a dark night sky.


Fortunately there are programs such as the River Murray International Dark Sky reserve which have been founded to protect and preserve places with dark, unpolluted night skies.
